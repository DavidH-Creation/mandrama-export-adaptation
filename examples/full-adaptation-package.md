# Example: Full Adaptation Package

## User Ask

```text
Package this Chinese web drama for international release.

Source:
- A medical intern is scapegoated after a hospital scandal engineered by her mentor
- She uncovers a corruption network tied to a private biotech investor
- Her ex is now the investor's public spokesman
- Tone: grounded thriller with romance undercurrent

Goals:
- Strong export pitch
- Sponsor-safe positioning
- Format recommendation
- Pilot rewrite direction
```

## Example Output

### 1. Export Viability Verdict

```text
- Verdict: High export potential
- Best market: US / UK / Western Europe thriller audiences
- Best language package: English-led with localized subtitle layers for secondary markets
- Best visual format: 3D MetaHuman
- Core reason: Medical conspiracy, betrayal, and moral compromise are highly portable hooks. The premise can be adapted into globally legible hospital and biotech politics with relatively little culture-friction, and the grounded thriller tone benefits from realism more than stylization.
```

### 2. Reasoning Table

| Signal | Type | Confidence | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Medical scandal and corruption are globally legible | fact | high | Reduces adaptation burden |
| A realism-first package will strengthen premium positioning | inference | medium | Supports 3D MetaHuman over anime-style execution |
| Sponsor-safe framing is possible if patient exploitation is handled carefully | inference | medium | Preserves monetization upside |
| The user has access to a pipeline capable of consistent 3D performance | assumption | low | Must be validated before locking format |

### 3. Adaptation Memo

- Preserve: institutional betrayal, career jeopardy, romantic distrust, conspiracy escalation
- Replace: local hospital hierarchy shorthand with internationally legible compliance, funding, and PR pressure
- Simplify: any side plots built on domestic celebrity cameo logic or local bureaucracy
- Intensify: the intern's evidence trail and the ex-lover's complicity
- Remove entirely: exposition that requires prior knowledge of Chinese medical licensing culture

### 4. Market Strategy Recommendation

**Chosen route:** Anglophone premium export

**Justification:**
- Audience fit: Thriller audiences in English-speaking markets already understand hospital scandal and biotech power games
- Monetization fit: English-first packaging gives the strongest pitch for premium YouTube or platform-facing proof of concept
- Cost: Higher dubbing cost than subtitle-first, but lower rewrite burden than a heavily stylized fandom package
- Production risk: Moderate if the team can maintain realistic acting quality in 3D

**Rejected routes and why:**
- Performance-led subculture export: Not stylized enough to justify fandom-first positioning
- Region-first localized launch: No evidence yet of a stronger first-market advantage outside Anglophone distribution
- Multilingual layered release: Better as a second-stage expansion after the core package is proven

### 5. Rewritten Project Package

#### International Logline

> After a young medical intern is framed for a deadly hospital cover-up, she risks her career and her last trusted relationship to expose the biotech empire buying the truth.

#### Revised Title Options

1. White Coat Fallout
2. Code of Silence
3. The Evidence Ward

#### Revised Character Bios

- **Mina Hart** - Protagonist / underestimated insider / rising whistleblower. A gifted intern whose instinct for detail becomes the only thing standing between a buried scandal and the people profiting from it.
- **Elias Voss** - Love interest / compromised public face / morally split ex. Once Mina's closest ally, now the polished spokesman for the investors tied to the scandal.
- **Dr. Rowan Vale** - Mentor-antagonist / prestige authority / smiling predator. A celebrated surgeon who treats institutional power as a shield against accountability.

#### Revised Setting Notes

Set in a private urban teaching hospital under media scrutiny, where donor money, legal exposure, and biotech influence shape every decision.

#### Rewritten Pilot Summary

The pilot opens with a patient emergency that collapses into public chaos when a treatment protocol fails under suspicious circumstances. Mina, the lowest-status person in the room, is quickly positioned as the disposable scapegoat while her mentor controls the narrative.

As internal investigators close in, Mina notices that chart edits and device logs do not match the official story. Her attempt to get help leads her back to Elias, now speaking for the investor group backing the hospital. By the end of the episode, Mina realizes the scandal is not a mistake. It is a revenue-protection operation.

#### Episode Hook Map

| Episode | Hook / Cold Open | Turn | Cliffhanger |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Patient crash during a live-demo procedure | Mina finds the record mismatch | She sees Elias at the hospital press briefing |
| 2 | Deleted surveillance footage | A nurse witness disappears | Mina receives an anonymous drive |
| 3 | Elias offers a private deal | The drive implicates the mentor | Mina learns the deaths may be intentional |

### 6. Dialogue Sample

**Source meaning:** Her mentor warns her to stay quiet because powerful people are protecting the hospital.

**Literal translation:** If you keep speaking, you will understand what kind of people you are offending.

**Localized line:** "Keep pushing this and you won't be fighting a hospital. You'll be fighting everyone who owns it."

**Why it works:** It preserves the threat while making the power structure instantly legible to an English-speaking audience.

### 7. Packaging Assets

- Title angle: prestige medical conspiracy
- Thumbnail text options: "She Found the Real Chart", "They Framed the Wrong Intern", "The Hospital Owns the Truth"
- Opening hook variants: "I didn't kill that patient. But someone needed me to take the fall."

### 8. Risk Register

- Legal realism risk: Medical procedure details may need simplification to avoid false precision
- Sponsor risk: Patient harm framing must stay serious, not exploitative
- Format risk: 3D close-up acting quality must clear thriller expectations

### 9. Next-Step Production Plan

#### Immediate Rewrite Tasks

- Rebuild the mentor's leverage around funding, liability, and compliance pressure
- Clarify the ex-lover's role in the cover-up before episode 2

#### Casting / Dubbing Notes

- Mina needs restrained intensity, not melodrama
- Elias should sound polished enough to be trusted before he is doubted

#### Art Direction Notes

- Keep the hospital premium but cold
- Use glass, reflective surfaces, and controlled lighting to reinforce institutional opacity

#### Edit / Subtitle Notes

- Maintain thriller pacing with short expository exchanges
- Subtitle layers should preserve procedural clarity over literal phrasing
