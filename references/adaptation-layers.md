# Adaptation Layers

Apply these 6 layers in order during Phase 3 (narrative adaptation). Each layer rewrites the source material at a different level of abstraction.

---

## Layer 1 — One-Line Sell

Rewrite the logline into a globally legible pitch. The logline must work as a single sentence that hooks an international audience.

**Bad:**
> Daughter of a rich family was framed by a rival and forced into a marriage contract.

**Better:**
> After a public betrayal destroys her future, a disinherited heiress weaponizes a fake marriage to take back her name, her company, and the man who sold her out.

**Rules:**
- Lead with conflict, not setup
- Include stakes and agency
- Avoid culture-locked terms that need footnotes
- Make the protagonist's goal clear in one breath

---

## Layer 2 — Character Archetype Translation

Map source character roles to globally legible archetypes. Do not translate the label literally — translate the *function*.

See [`archetype-glossary.md`](archetype-glossary.md) for the full lookup table.

**Key examples:**

| Source | NOT this | Instead, translate functionally as |
|---|---|---|
| 霸总 (bà zǒng) | "domineering CEO" | cold founder / ruthless heir / emotionally armored billionaire / obsessive corporate predator (depends on tone) |
| 白月光 (bái yuè guāng) | "white moonlight" | idealized first love / untouchable past obsession |
| 绿茶 (lǜ chá) | "green tea" | manipulative ingenue / performative innocent rival |
| 赘婿 (zhuì xù) | "live-in son-in-law" | dependent husband / outsider husband / strategic marriage underdog (depends on context) |

**Rules:**
- Map by emotional function, not literal meaning
- Adjust archetype based on story tone (prestige thriller vs romance vs comedy)
- Ensure the archetype reads correctly in the target market without a glossary

---

## Layer 3 — Social Logic Rewrite

Replace culture-locked incentives with export-legible incentives. The underlying emotional logic should be equivalent even if the surface mechanism changes.

| Source Mechanism | Export-Legible Replacement |
|---|---|
| Parental arranged marriage pressure | Inheritance control, PR scandal management, family empire obligation |
| School ranking shame | Elite access pressure, scholarship stakes, career gatekeeping |
| Local gossip ecology | Social media scandal, board politics, influencer exposure, viral humiliation |
| Face (面子) dynamics | Public reputation, media exposure, professional credibility |
| Filial piety obligation | Family loyalty, estate control, generational debt |
| Workplace hierarchy abuse | Corporate power dynamics, HR politics, mentorship betrayal |

**Rules:**
- Replace the *mechanism*, keep the *emotion*
- Every social pressure must be explained by the scene, not assumed by the audience
- If the replacement feels forced, the emotional engine may need reshaping — go back to Layer 1

---

## Layer 4 — Dialogue Rewrite

High-level rules for dialogue adaptation. For the full localization engine, see [`dialogue-localization-engine.md`](dialogue-localization-engine.md).

- Remove direct-translated stiffness
- Shorten exposition
- Reduce repetitive emotional labeling ("I'm so angry!" → show the anger)
- Make subtext do more work
- Preserve intensity, not phrasing
- Lines must sound actable in target language

---

## Layer 5 — Episode Architecture

For short-form export, enforce these structural rules:

- **Cold open hook** in first 1-3 seconds — visual or dialogue that creates immediate tension
- **Emotional or plot turn** by 15-25 seconds — something changes, escalates, or reverses
- **Cliff / reveal / reversal** before episode end — audience must need the next episode
- **Episode title** promises conflict, not description ("She Knows" not "The Meeting")

**Pacing rules:**
- Cut exposition that delays the hook
- Front-load conflict, back-load explanation
- End mid-scene, not post-resolution
- Each episode must be self-contained enough to hook a new viewer but connected enough to retain a returning one

---

## Layer 6 — Platform Packaging

Generate packaging assets for platform distribution:

- **Thumbnail text variants** — 5 options, short and punchy, designed for vertical format
- **English title variants** — 5 options, genre-coded, searchable
- **Opening hook variants** — 3 options for the first line of dialogue or narration
- **Sponsor-safe notes** — what's clean for brand integration
- **Sponsor-risk notes** — what could make brands nervous

See [`../templates/packaging-sheet.md`](../templates/packaging-sheet.md) for the output template.
